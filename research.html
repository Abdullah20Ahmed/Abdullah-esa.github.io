Research Program

Computational Genomics Laboratory

Our lab develops and applies computational methods to understand how genes are regulated in development and disease. We focus on single-cell genomics, spatial transcriptomics, and machine learning approaches to model gene regulatory networks.

Research Areas

Single-Cell Genomics

Developing algorithms for cell-type identification and trajectory inference from scRNA-seq data

Neurodevelopment

Understanding gene regulatory networks in human brain development using organoid models

Gene Regulatory Networks

Inferring causal networks from multi-omic data using deep learning approaches

Spatial Transcriptomics

Mapping gene expression in tissue context to understand cell-cell interactions

Current Projects

2024-2026
NIH R01: Single-cell mapping of neurodevelopmental disorders
Principal Investigator · $1.2M
Integrating single-cell genomics and machine learning to identify regulatory mechanisms in autism spectrum disorder. We are generating single-cell multi-omic data from human brain organoids and developing computational methods to identify dysregulated gene regulatory networks.
2023-2025
NSF CAREER: Interpretable AI for gene regulation
Principal Investigator · $650K
Developing explainable deep learning models to predict enhancer-promoter interactions across cell types. This project combines transformer architectures with attention mechanisms to provide biological insights into gene regulation.
2022-2024
Spatial transcriptomics in cancer immunology
Collaborator · Dana-Farber Cancer Institute
Mapping tumor-immune interactions in melanoma using spatial transcriptomics and computational modeling. We are developing methods to identify cell-cell communication networks from spatial data.
2022-2024
Single-cell atlas of human brain development
Co-Investigator · Allen Institute Collaboration
Creating a comprehensive single-cell transcriptomic atlas of human brain development from early gestation to adulthood. Our lab leads the computational analysis of gene regulatory dynamics.
