Sarah Chen, PhD

Computational Biologist · Systems Genomics
My research integrates machine learning with high-throughput genomic data to understand gene regulation in development and disease. I develop computational frameworks that bridge molecular biology and clinical applications.

I lead the Computational Genomics Lab at Northeastern University, where my team builds interpretable models for single-cell and spatial transcriptomics. Our work has been funded by NIH and NSF, with applications in cancer immunology and neurodevelopment.
